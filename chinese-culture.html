Chinese Culture

Traditions and Customs

Chinese culture is characterized by its rich traditions such as Confucianism, Taoism, and Buddhism. Traditional practices include Chinese calligraphy, painting, and the art of tea-making. The Chinese New Year is a major celebration, marked by dragon dances and family reunions. Ancestor worship involves paying respect to ancestors through offerings and ceremonies. At the same time, filial piety requires children to care for and honour their parents as part of their family bond. Superstitions and Feng Shui are also an integral part of Chinese culture.

Festivals

Notable Chinese festivals include the Lunar New Year, Mid-Autumn Festival, and Dragon Boat Festival. The Lunar New Year, also known as Spring Festival, is celebrated with fireworks, family gatherings, and special foods like dumplings and rice cakes.

Arts and Crafts

Chinese arts include traditional crafts such as silk weaving, paper cutting, and porcelain making. Chinese silk art is famous for its intricate designs and luxurious textures, while porcelain is renowned for its quality and beauty.
