Indigenous Culture

Traditions and Customs

The indigenous cultures of Malaysia, including the Orang Asli and the Dayak, have unique traditions and customs that are deeply connected to nature. Their practices include traditional ceremonies, dances, and rituals that honor their ancestors and the natural world.

Festivals

Indigenous festivals often celebrate natural cycles and ancestral heritage. Festivals such as the Gawai Dayak and Kaamatan are significant, involving communal feasts, traditional performances, and rituals to mark the harvest season and other important events.

Arts and Crafts

Indigenous arts include intricate beadwork, weaving, and carving. Traditional crafts are often used in ceremonies and are highly valued for their cultural significance and craftsmanship. These arts reflect the close relationship between indigenous communities and their environment.
